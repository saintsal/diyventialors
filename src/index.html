What should I do to help?

I am working on a breathing device

If your project is ready for in-hospital testing it is a priority. Please phone Nato directly on +359 885291378.

  1. Start a forum thread with a quick description of your project. Upload any files you have so others can quickly understand and learn from your progress so far.
  2. Post progress updates at least once per day. Treat it like a mini-blog, with problems, progress, requests for help. We will jump on those for you.
  3. Post your medical and engineering questions as soon as they come up, so we can direct qualified people to answer them faster.
  4. Keep an eye on which designs are getting the farthest ahead. You may have already solved one of their problems, so can save them time, or you may decide to switch to their design if it serves need faster.

I want to offer my help

  1. Register yourself on the volunteer list, so teams who need you can find you.
  2. Read over the different team's progress updates, and proactively get them what they need.

Medical background?

Please jump on the forum to answer questions our team have for medical staff.

Can you make devices?

You can answer technical questions to unblock teams quickly.

If you are an engineer or hardware person, the best thing you can do is pick one of the leading designs, and start working on it yourself, right now. Why? The more eyeballs we have working on those designs, the faster the hurdles and bugs are overcome. The needs are different in different places. Some need BVM automation, others only intubation, other places need oxygen therapy. So to help you decide, join this discussion.

But please pick one today and just start. We'll help you get what you need.

Software person?

Software to run the devices is likely to be the hardest part, so if you have experience with embedded systems or robust, low-error languages, start to get your head around this challenge. (the NXP model documentation might be a good place to start.)

Rules

  • Prioritise action. Act independently. The worst thing you can do is not start, or wait around. We have to priortise support for people taking action themselves. You start, we follow.
  • No need for permission. We forgive you in advance! You move fast, we'll clean up behind you.
  • Work in the open. Share your wins, lessons learned, problems as soon as they happen. That saves everyone else time, and gets you solutions faster. Post forum updates on all little advances to keep everyone up-to-date by the hour.

This project run 100% by volunteers and is non-profit. It is a collaboration between Source Institute - a peer learning skunkworks, Sofia Tech Park - Bulgaria's largest science park, and BESCO - The Bulgarian Startup Association. It is spearheaded by Salim Virani and Nato Stefanov (also volunteering), with support from many other volunteers who've just stepped up in a time of need.

We are doing this to help people in all countries, but basing out of our resources here in Bulgaria for fast action.
